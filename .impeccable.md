## Design Context

### Users
The Shree Ayurveda website serves individuals seeking authentic Ayurvedic healing treatments, primarily adults aged 25-65 who are interested in holistic wellness, traditional medicine, and natural healing approaches. Users visit the site to learn about treatments, book consultations, and understand the benefits of Ayurvedic practices for chronic conditions, stress relief, and overall wellness.

### Brand Personality
Natural, Trustworthy, Authentic
- Voice: Warm, knowledgeable, and reassuring
- Tone: Healing-focused, educational, and compassionate
- Emotional goals: Instill confidence in traditional healing, promote feelings of calm and well-being, establish credibility as an authentic Ayurvedic center

### Aesthetic Direction
Enhanced Green, Organic, Refined Naturalism
- Visual tone: Rich, noticeable green-focused palette with healing teal and turmeric orange accents
- References: Organic skincare brands, wellness retreats, natural apothecaries, holistic health centers, herbal medicine apothecaries
- Anti-references: Clinical medical websites, overly corporate designs, dark mode health sites, generic template designs, white/empty feeling interfaces
- Theme: Light mode only - enhanced green tones for wellness/healing context where users seek connection with nature and healing

### Design Principles
1. **Enhanced Green Sophistication**: Use richer, more noticeable green tones as the primary background to evoke freshness, nature, and healing while avoiding the "empty white" feeling
2. **Subtle Accents**: Apply healing teal and turmeric orange as purposeful accent colors for interactive elements and visual hierarchy
3. **Typography Hierarchy**: Pair Playfair Display (elegant serif) for headings with Manrope (clean sans-serif) for body text to create sophistication and readability
4. **Whitespace and Rhythm**: Employ varied spacing with a 4px scale to create visual breathing room and guide the eye naturally through content
5. **Interactive Feedback**: Provide subtle, meaningful hover states using green tones that feel responsive without being distracting
6. **Authentic Details**: Incorporate traditional Ayurvedic symbols and imagery thoughtfully to reinforce cultural authenticity
7. **Accessibility First**: Ensure all interactive elements meet touch target requirements and color contrast ratios for inclusive access
